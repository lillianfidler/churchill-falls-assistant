KEY FACTS ABOUT THE 2024 MOU:

PARTIES & DATE:
• Signed December 12, 2024
• Between Newfoundland & Labrador Hydro (NLH), Hydro-Québec (HQ), and Churchill Falls (Labrador) Corporation Limited (CF(L)Co)
• Replaces the controversial 1969 contract that expires August 31, 2041

MAIN OBJECTIVES:
• Terminate and replace 1969 contract with new 51-year agreements (2025-2075)
• Create 50-50 revenue sharing between NL and Quebec
• Develop three major expansion projects totaling ~3,900 MW new capacity
• Give NL access to 1,990 MW, Quebec access to minimum 7,200 MW

THREE DEVELOPMENT PROJECTS:

1. CF Units Upgrade (2028-2038)
   • Upgrade all turbine-generator units
   • Add approximately 550 MW capacity
   • Cost-plus pricing with 2% annual revenue escalation

2. CF Expansion Project (2035)
   • New powerhouse adjacent to existing facility
   • Add approximately 1,100 MW capacity
   • Owned and operated by CF(L)Co
   • Cost-plus pricing with 2% annual escalation

3. Gull Island Project (2034-2035)
   • New hydroelectric facility 100 km southwest of Happy Valley-Goose Bay
   • Add approximately 2,250 MW capacity
   • 60% NLH ownership, 40% HQ ownership (joint venture)
   • Half commissioned 2034, half 2035

PRICING STRUCTURE:

Existing Churchill Falls Plant:
• New market-based block pricing structure
• Forecasted net present value of $33.8 billion in payments to CF(L)Co from HQ over contract term
• Retroactive to January 1, 2025
• Dramatic improvement over 1969 rates (was ~0.2¢/kWh, now market-based)

New Capacity:
• Cost-plus pricing for all new projects
• 2% per annum revenue escalation
• 75% debt / 25% equity capital structure for expansions
• Return on equity: 10-year Canada bonds + 500 basis points (floor 8%, ceiling 9%)

CONTRACT TERMS:
• Duration: 51 years (January 1, 2025 to December 31, 2075)
• NO automatic renewal (major change from 1969 contract)
• NL has recapture rights - can take back power for provincial use
• Volume blocks expire 2050 and 2060 - NL has first option to purchase

FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLES:
• Alignment of strategic and economic interests
• Relationships with Indigenous communities
• Environmental approvals and standards
• Balanced governance structure
• Transparent communication and collaboration

REVENUE ALLOCATION:
• NL will receive approximately 360 MW and 1.92 TWh from Development Projects
• 225 MW and 1.2 TWh from Gull Island Project specifically
• If NL doesn't need contracted volumes, HQ must purchase unused volumes

TRANSMISSION:
• New transmission assets required in both Labrador and Quebec
• NL responsible for Labrador transmission
• Quebec responsible for Quebec transmission

TIMELINE SUMMARY:
• 2025: New agreements retroactively take effect
• 2028-2038: CF Units Upgrade rollout
• 2034: Gull Island half commissioned
• 2035: CF Expansion + remaining Gull Island capacity
• 2041: Original 1969 contract expires (replaced by this MOU)
• 2050 & 2060: Major volume blocks expire with NL recapture options
• 2075: All agreements expire

KEY DIFFERENCES FROM 1969 CONTRACT:
• Market-based pricing vs. fixed low rates
• 51 years vs. 65 years with automatic renewal
• 50-50 revenue sharing vs. Quebec capturing vast majority
• NL has recapture rights and future control options
• Joint venture ownership on new developments
• No automatic renewal clause

ECONOMIC IMPACT:
• $33.8 billion NPV for existing Churchill Falls over contract term
• Additional billions from new developments
• 2% annual escalation ensures revenues grow with inflation
• NL finally receives fair market value for electricity
