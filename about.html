About This Resource

The Churchill Falls Information Assistant is a research tool designed to provide accurate, source-based information about the Churchill Falls hydroelectric project and the December 2024 Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) between Newfoundland and Labrador and Quebec.

This system was created to address the need for accessible, reliable information about complex hydroelectric agreements and their economic implications. By consolidating official documents, expert analyses, and historical context into a single searchable resource, it serves students, researchers, journalists, policymakers, and the general public.

Important: All responses are drawn exclusively from this system's curated knowledge base. The assistant does not use external searches or general internet information. Every answer is based solely on verified sources including:

  • Official Documents: The December 12, 2024 MOU, the 2002 Gull Island Framework Agreement, and the 1969 Churchill Falls contract
  • Economic Analyses: Dr. Doug May's comprehensive 6-video series and written analyses
  • Expert Assessments: Wade Locke's economic critique and James Feehan's academic research
  • Corporate Reports: Annual reports from Churchill Falls Labrador Corporation, Hydro-Québec, and NL Hydro (2024)
  • Historical Context: Documents tracing the development of Churchill River hydro projects from 1949-2007

What You Can Ask: Questions about pricing structures, project proposals, economic impacts, historical agreements, financial analyses, and the implications of the 2024 MOU for both Newfoundland and Labrador and Quebec.

Citation Standards: The assistant provides specific citations including document names, dates, and direct links to video analyses when applicable, ensuring full transparency and verifiability of all information.

About Dr. Doug May

Dr. Doug May is an economist and Professor Emeritus at Memorial University of Newfoundland. His research focuses on energy economics, regional economic development, and public policy analysis with particular expertise in hydroelectric agreements and their economic impacts.

Dr. May has created the most comprehensive independent economic assessment of the December 2024 MOU through his 6-part video analysis series and supporting written materials. His analyses examine present value calculations, discount rate methodology, Quebec's electricity situation, pricing structures, water royalties, and financial concepts—all grounded in economic theory and publicly available data.

His work is characterized by transparent methodology, clear explanations accessible to general audiences, balanced presentation of multiple perspectives, and explicit acknowledgment of information gaps and analytical limitations. All of Dr. May's video analyses are available on YouTube and are fully integrated into this assistant's knowledge base.

Methodology and Limitations

How the Assistant Works

The Churchill Falls Information Assistant uses advanced language technology (Claude by Anthropic) to analyze and synthesize information from the curated knowledge base. When you ask a question:

  1. The system searches all source documents for relevant information
  2. Multiple sources are cross-referenced to ensure accuracy
  3. Responses include specific citations with document names, dates, and links
  4. Different analytical perspectives are presented when they exist in the source materials
  5. The system clearly indicates when information is not available in the knowledge base

What the Assistant Can Do

  • Explain technical economic and legal concepts in accessible language
  • Compare different analytical perspectives (e.g., Dr. May vs. Wade Locke)
  • Provide specific citations with dates and video links
  • Answer follow-up questions and maintain conversation context
  • Adapt explanation depth based on user expertise level

Limitations

  • No External Sources: The assistant does not access information beyond its knowledge base. It cannot provide updates about events after the documents were created.
  • No Predictions: The assistant does not make predictions about future outcomes or events.
  • No Legal/Financial Advice: Responses are educational only and not professional advice.
  • Document-Bound: Analysis is limited to what is explicitly stated in the source materials.
