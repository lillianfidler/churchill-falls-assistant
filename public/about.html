About This Assistant

Expert analysis and comprehensive information about Churchill Falls hydroelectric projects and energy agreements.

Welcome

This information assistant provides accurate, source-based information about the Churchill Falls hydroelectric project and the December 2024 Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) between Newfoundland and Labrador and Quebec. By consolidating official documents, expert analyses, and historical context into a single resource, it serves students, researchers, journalists, policymakers, and the general public.

Important: All responses are drawn exclusively from this system's curated knowledge base. The assistant does not use external searches or general internet information. Every answer is based solely on verified sources.

About Dr. Doug May

Dr. Doug May is an economist and Professor Emeritus at Memorial University of Newfoundland. His research focuses on energy economics, regional economic development, and public policy analysis with particular expertise in hydroelectric agreements and their economic impacts.

Dr. May has created the most comprehensive independent economic assessment of the December 2024 MOU through his 6-part video analysis series and supporting written materials. His analyses examine present value calculations, discount rate methodology, Quebec's electricity situation, pricing structures, water royalties, and financial concepts‚Äîall grounded in economic theory and publicly available data.

How It Works

The Churchill Falls Information Assistant uses advanced language technology (Claude by Anthropic) to analyze and synthesize information from the curated knowledge base. When you ask a question:

  1. The system searches all source documents for relevant information
  2. Multiple sources are cross-referenced to ensure accuracy
  3. Responses include specific citations with document names and dates
  4. Different analytical perspectives are presented when they exist
  5. The system clearly indicates when information is not available

What the Assistant Can Do

  • Explain technical economic and legal concepts in accessible language
  • Compare different analytical perspectives (Dr. May, Wade Locke, government positions)
  • Provide specific citations with dates and video links
  • Answer follow-up questions and maintain conversation context
  • Adapt explanation depth based on your needs

Limitations

  • No External Sources: The assistant does not access information beyond its knowledge base.
  • No Predictions: The assistant does not make predictions about future outcomes.
  • No Legal/Financial Advice: Responses are educational only, not professional advice.
  • Document-Bound: Analysis is limited to what is explicitly stated in source materials.
